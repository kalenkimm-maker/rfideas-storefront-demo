Why 13.56 MHz

Smart Cards Are Smart for a Reason

Unlike 125 kHz prox cards, 13.56 MHz credentials use cryptographic protocols — mutual authentication, encrypted data exchange, and anti-cloning protections. The card proves it's genuine; the reader proves it's authorized. Password-level authentication, badge-level convenience.

Mutual Authentication The reader and card authenticate each other cryptographically — eliminating cloned-card attacks that defeat prox systems.
AES-128 Encryption DESFire EV3 and iClass Seos use AES-128 — the same encryption standard used for classified government data.
Full Audit Trail Smart card transactions generate cryptographically verifiable logs — meeting HIPAA, SOX, and FISMA audit requirements.
